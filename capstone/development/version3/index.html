Menstruation Over Time

Can you share your experience with menstruation, and how menstruation is discussed during your time?

Discuss? It was never discussed... People considered it unclean and shameful.

Folks thought our blood could kill crops or make mirrors go dull.

Some even believed we could curse someone just by looking at them during our time.

To stop the bleeding, we tried all sorts of odd things, like strapping dried toads around their waists or using old rags.

Ugh. It is definitely not fun. I personally get bad cramps and mood swings during those days.

People online are more open now, like posting tips for handling cramps, product reviews, and sharing their stories.

But my friends and I still feel awkward talking about it openly, especially around male family members.

Even now, when I need to change my hygiene product, I instinctively hide it up my sleeve on the way to a public restroom.

Oh, periods? They’re just a normal part of life. Nothing to be ashamed of!

Menstrual products are very affordable and hygienic to use. I get free products from my hospital every month too.

We’ve got smart care systems that track everything and adjust to your body in real time, so managing your cycle is super easy.

Everyone is educated early on to understand the purpose and importance of menstruation. It is a pretty common and open topic nowadays.

Periods are still treated like a taboo topic. People whisper about it or avoid talking altogether.

Access to menstrual products is limited. Prices have gone up and there's little support for people who can't afford them.

Schools barely educate students on menstrual health. Many grow up ashamed or misinformed.

I often feel like I have to hide what I'm going through, which is exhausting. The stigma never really went away.
